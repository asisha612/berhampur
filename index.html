About Berhampur & Gopalpur

Berhampur, known as the Silk City, is renowned for its silk sarees, temples, and rich cultural heritage. It is a bustling city with a blend of tradition and modernity.

Gopalpur, a serene beach town, offers pristine beaches, a tranquil environment, and is a perfect getaway for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

History of Berhampur

Berhampur, often referred to as the Silk City, has a rich history that dates back to ancient times. The city, located in the Ganjam district of Odisha, India, has been a significant center for trade and commerce for centuries. Berhampur's strategic location near the eastern coast of India made it a crucial trading hub for silk, spices, and other goods, connecting various parts of India and even other countries.

The city derives its name from 'Brahmapur', which means the abode of Brahma, one of the principal deities in Hinduism. Over time, the name evolved to Berhampur. The city's history is closely tied to its role in the silk trade, which flourished under various dynasties, including the Mauryas and Guptas.

Berhampur is also known for its cultural heritage, with numerous temples and festivals that attract visitors from all over the region. The Thakurani Yatra, a biennial festival, is one of the most celebrated events in the city, showcasing its vibrant traditions and community spirit.

Today, Berhampur is a bustling urban center that seamlessly blends its rich historical past with modern development. The city continues to be a major hub for silk production, and its markets are famous for exquisite silk sarees and other textiles. As a gateway to the picturesque beaches of Gopalpur, Berhampur offers a unique combination of cultural richness and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Odisha.

Top Destinations

Ramalingeswar Park

Ramalingeswar Park is a serene oasis in Berhampur, perfect for families and nature lovers. It features lush greenery, vibrant flowers, walking paths, and a children's playground. The park hosts cultural events and yoga sessions, offering a peaceful retreat from city life..

Taratarini Temple

Taratarini Temple, located on the banks of the River Rushikulya, is a revered pilgrimage site dedicated to the twin goddesses Tara and Tarini. Perched on a hilltop, the temple offers stunning views and is a center of spiritual activities, especially during the annual Chaitra Mela.

Berhampur Markets

Explore the bustling markets of Berhampur, where you can find a variety of local products including traditional silk sarees, textiles, and other handicrafts. The markets are vibrant and offer a unique shopping experience.

Travel Tips

Travel Tips for Berhampur & Gopalpur

  • Best Time to Visit: The ideal time to visit Berhampur and Gopalpur is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities.
  • Local Transport: Both cities are well-connected by taxis, auto-rickshaws, and buses. Renting a bike or car is also a popular option for exploring the area at your own pace.
  • Local Cuisine: Don't miss out on trying local delicacies. Berhampur is famous for its delicious street food and traditional Odia dishes.
  • Safety Tips: Both cities are generally safe for tourists. However, it's always good to stay vigilant and follow basic safety precautions, especially when exploring unfamiliar areas.
  • Language: Odia is the local language, but English and Hindi are also widely understood.
